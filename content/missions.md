My algorithms don't live in papers alone — they run inside NASA missions, turning faint microwave measurements into the soil-moisture, snow, and freeze–thaw products people actually use. Here is the work, mission by mission.

## SMAP {#smap}

The mission that defined my career. On NASA's L-band Soil Moisture Active Passive mission, I develop the algorithms that read soil moisture from radar and radiometer measurements — and the freeze–thaw detection that began on Aquarius became an operational SMAP product. My snow work and the joint SMAP–NISAR retrieval algorithm both trace back to this lineage.

See the science threads behind it: [SMAP + NISAR soil moisture](/project/smap-nisar-soil-moisture/) and [Freeze–thaw](/project/freeze-thaw-vod/).

## NISAR {#nisar}

The NISAR era carries the L-band soil-moisture work forward. I contribute to the joint SMAP–NISAR retrieval algorithm and the ROSE-L–class L-band SAR approach, and to repeat-pass InSAR methods for tracking seasonal snow.

Related work: [SMAP + NISAR soil moisture](/project/smap-nisar-soil-moisture/) and [Snow & InSAR SWE](/project/snow-insar-swe/).

## CYGNSS {#cygnss}

Soil moisture from a different kind of signal — the navigation-satellite signals that reflect off the land surface (GNSS-R). I built JPL's Level-3 soil-moisture product from CYGNSS, and extended it to vegetation optical depth from CYGNSS v3.

Related work: [GNSS-R for land](/project/gnss-r-land/).

## Aquarius {#aquarius}

Where my freeze–thaw work began: L-band detection of whether the land surface is frozen or thawed — the signal that marks the onset of spring and the timing of frost. This early-career thread grew into the operational SMAP freeze–thaw science product.

Related work: [Freeze–thaw](/project/freeze-thaw-vod/).

## P-band — in preparation {#pband}

A mission concept still taking shape. Lower-frequency P-band reaches deeper into soil and vegetation than the L-band I work with today, opening new terrain for the same wave-physics-first approach. More as the concept matures.
