Satellites read soil moisture, snowpack, and frozen ground from the faint microwaves that scatter off the land. The physics of how the wave meets the terrain is the **backbone of every measurement** — and it's the part I own.

My work runs the whole chain: from first-principles wave physics to the mission-ready algorithms behind SMAP, NISAR, and CYGNSS.

The direction I'm pushing now is **closing the loop** — making each modeling assumption explicit, putting honest uncertainty on every retrieved value, and letting validation data improve the equations themselves. No one field can do that alone, and that's the interesting part.
