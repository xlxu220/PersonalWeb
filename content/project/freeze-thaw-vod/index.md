## Why it matters

The moment the land surface freezes or thaws is a switch for the whole ecosystem: it sets when plants start growing in spring, when soil releases or stores carbon, and when frost threatens crops. Across the vast boreal and Arctic regions, there's no way to track that switch from the ground — but it's visible from space, because liquid water and ice interact with microwaves very differently. When the ground freezes, the signal changes sharply.

## What I work on

I develop the algorithms that detect **landscape freeze/thaw state** from L-band measurements, and — just as importantly — the **validation** that proves the detection is real and not an artifact. This thread began with the **Aquarius** mission, where I demonstrated freeze/thaw detection from L-band radar, and matured into an operational **SMAP** science product (the L3 freeze/thaw data record), now used to study spring onset and frost across the high latitudes.
